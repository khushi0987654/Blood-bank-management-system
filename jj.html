What is Blood Donation?

Blood donation is a voluntary procedure that can help save the lives of others. Donors provide a pint of their blood, which is processed and used for patients in need, such as those undergoing surgery, victims of accidents, or those with certain medical conditions.

Importance of Blood Donation

Blood donation is crucial for saving lives. It is essential for patients in need of surgeries, those with chronic diseases like cancer, and for victims of trauma. Blood cannot be manufactured, and thus donations from healthy individuals are vital.

Who Can Donate Blood?

Individuals who meet the following criteria can donate blood:

  • Be in good general health.
  • Be at least 18 years old.
  • Weigh at least 50 kg.
  • Have no major medical conditions.
  • Have not donated blood in the last 3 months.

The Blood Donation Process

The process of donating blood is simple and involves the following steps:

  • Registration: Donors provide their basic information.
  • Health Check: A quick check-up to ensure the donor is fit for donation.
  • Donation: The actual blood donation takes about 10 minutes.
  • Recovery: Rest and refreshments for 10-15 minutes.
